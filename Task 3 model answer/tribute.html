Ada Lovelace

The First Computer Programmer


Timeline

  • 1815: Ada Lovelace was born in London, England on 10th December 1815.
  • Mid-1830s: Lovelace collaborated with Charles Babbage around the mid-1830s on a mechanical general-purpose computer called the Analytical Engine which handled complex calculations.
  • 1843: She recognized the broader applications of the Analytical Engine and published the first algorithm to calculate Bernoulli numbers.
  • 1852: At the age of 36 on 27th November, Lovelace died from uterine cancer.

After Life

  • Lovelace's contributions were not widely recognized during her lifetime but gained appreciation in the 20th century.
  • Ada Lovelace Day is celebrated annually on the second Tuesday of October to honor her contributions in STEM fields.

Ada Lovelace's Quotes

The analytical engine has no pretensions to originate anything. It can do whatever we know how to order it to perform.


Understand well as I may, my comprehension can only be an infinitesimal fraction of all I want to understand.


That brain of mine is something more than merely mortal; as time will show.
